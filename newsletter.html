Attack-SAM: Towards Evaluating Adversarial Robustness of Segment Anything Model

2023-05-01 15:08:17+00:00

['Chenshuang Zhang', 'Chaoning Zhang', 'Taegoo Kang', 'Donghun Kim', 'Sung-Ho Bae', 'In So Kweon']

In recent years, the Segment Anything Model (SAM) has been gaining popularity in the field of computer vision due to its exceptional performance in various downstream tasks. However, like most machine learning models, SAM is vulnerable to adversarial attacks, where imperceptible perturbations can cause the model to make incorrect predictions. This vulnerability raises concerns when using deep learning models for security-sensitive applications. To address this issue, a research team conducted a study called Attack-SAM, aimed at evaluating the adversarial robustness of the SAM model. As far as the team knows, their work is the first to assess how to attack SAM with adversarial examples comprehensively. The study found that SAM is susceptible to white-box attacks, where the attacker has complete knowledge of the model's architecture and parameters. However, the model maintains robustness to some degree in the black-box setting, where the attacker lacks such knowledge. The results of this ongoing project are available through their Github repository: https://github.com/chenshuang-zhang/attack-sam. The findings of this study could contribute to the design and evaluation of more robust computer vision models for security-sensitive applications. As the field of computer vision moves towards foundation models, like in natural language processing, ensuring their robustness against adversarial attacks is critical.

Patent Mining by Extracting Functional Analysis Information Modelled As Graph Structure: A Patent Knowledge-base Collaborative Building Approach

2023-04-29 17:34:11+00:00

['Manal E. Helal']

Patent mining is an essential component of innovation, as it provides valuable information about design innovations. Various technologies, such as text mining, machine learning, natural language processing, and ontology-building techniques, are employed in patent mining. A research paper proposes an automated graph data modelling method for extracting functional representations for building a semantic database of patents related to mechanical designs. The proposed method has several characteristics that make it unique. Firstly, the schema-free nature of the proposed graph modelling enables the ontology to evolve and generalise to upper ontologies across technology domains, specifying lower ontologies to more specific domains. Secondly, the graph modelling benefits from enhanced performance of deep queries across many levels of relationships and interactions and provides efficient storage. Thirdly, graph modelling enables visualisation libraries to use the graph data structure immediately, avoiding the need for graph extraction programs from relational databases. The paper uses the example of PatMine SolidWorks Add-in, which compares annotated CAD designs with patents and highlights overlapping design concepts, to demonstrate the proposed method. The patent annotation extracts its functional analysis, representing its structure as geometric feature interactions, while additional features like full-text search and semantic search of the PatMine patents database are available. Graph analytic methods and machine learning algorithms are enabled and can be implemented as plug-ins in future work. The proposed approach is expected to have a significant impact on patent mining by enabling the construction of knowledge bases collaboratively, reducing the complexity and cost of constructing databases, and improving precision in searching patents. It has the potential to transform the process of patent mining entirely. The paper affirms that the proposed graph data modelling method will be useful in generating and managing enormous amounts of patent data as it reduces the manual input needed to construct patent databases, reducing professional time and costs. The application of the proposed method is not limited to patent mining as it has the potential to improve any application of semantic analysis, functional analysis diagrams, graph data modelling, visualisation, similarity scoring, big data analytics, machine learning, artificial intelligence, and natural language processing.

pyBibX -- A Python Library for Bibliometric and Scientometric Analysis Powered with Artificial Intelligence Tools

2023-04-27 20:06:07+00:00

['Valdecy Pereira', 'Marcio Pereira Basilio', 'Carlos Henrique Tarjano Santos']

A new tool is available for bibliometric and scientometric analyses of raw data files from Scopus, Web of Science, and PubMed. The tool, pyBibX, is run on Python and is designed to seamlessly integrate state-of-the-art AI capabilities into core functionality. These include embedding vectors, text summarization, and topic modeling, utilizing models such as BERT, Sentence-BERT, and PEGASUS. The tool executes a comprehensive exploratory data analysis (EDA) and displays the resulting outcomes through visually appealing graphics. Additionally, network capabilities have been incorporated to achieve citation, collaboration, and similarity analyses. A demonstration of the tool reveals a growing interest in decision-making and fuzzy logic methodologies, with Collaboration Analysis identifying Canada and China as important centers of collaboration. AI analyses distinguished primary topics and also recognized chatGPT preeminence in text summarization. This is a promising end-to-end platform for bibliometric and scientometric analysis, particularly in the natural language processing field for the medical domain.

Harnessing the Power of LLMs in Practice: A Survey on ChatGPT and Beyond

['Jingfeng Yang', 'Hongye Jin', 'Ruixiang Tang', 'Xiaotian Han', 'Qizhang Feng', 'Haoming Jiang', 'Bing Yin', 'Xia Hu']

Large Language Models (LLMs) enable humans to interact with machines through natural language and Artificial Intelligence (AI) systems. However, the increasing amount of data available has posed significant challenges to NLP tasks, such as information overload and data inconsistency. To address these challenges, researchers have proposed LLMs that leverage immense amounts of training data to achieve promising performance in downstream tasks. To help practitioners navigate the complex process of deploying LLMs, researchers have reported a comprehensive and practical guide for implementing LLMs in various NLP tasks. The guide highlights the essential components of LLMs, including pre-training data, training data, and test data, and provides detailed use cases and non-use cases. The paper emphasizes the importance of data, the specific challenges associated with each NLP task, and the effects of spurious biases. Additionally, factors such as efficiency, cost, and latency, must be considered to ensure the successful deployment of LLMs in practice. However, the guide also highlights the limitations of LLMs, including potential issues with the quality of the data, which can result in biased or inaccurate predictions. Despite this limitation, the guide aims to provide a valuable resource for researchers and practitioners working with LLMs in NLP tasks. The authors also provide links to a curated list of practical guide resources, updated regularly to provide users with the latest information and best practices for deploying LLMs. Overall, this paper offers valuable insights and best practices for deploying LLMs in various NLP tasks, providing an excellent resource for researchers and practitioners alike.
