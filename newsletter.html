Attack-SAM: Towards Evaluating Adversarial Robustness of Segment Anything Model

2023-05-01 15:08:17+00:00

['Chenshuang Zhang', 'Chaoning Zhang', 'Taegoo Kang', 'Donghun Kim', 'Sung-Ho Bae', 'In So Kweon']

In the field of computer vision, the Segment Anything Model (SAM) has been making waves due to its exceptional performance on various downstream tasks in a zero-shot manner. However, as with many deep learning models, SAM is also vulnerable to adversarial examples. These are inputs that have been specially crafted to fool the model into making incorrect predictions with seemingly insignificant perturbations. In a recent study, researchers set out to evaluate the adversarial robustness of the SAM model. Their work is the first of its kind to conduct a comprehensive investigation into how to attack SAM with adversarial examples. The study highlights the importance of examining the susceptibility of foundation models like SAM to such attacks and the potential impact on security-sensitive applications that utilize deep learning models. The researchers found that SAM is vulnerable to white-box attacks, where the attacker has complete knowledge of the model's architecture and parameters. However, they noted that SAM maintains a certain degree of robustness in the black-box setting, where the attacker has limited knowledge of model details. The study is still ongoing, and the researchers plan to update their findings on their GitHub repository, https://github.com/chenshuang-zhang/attack-sam. The work emphasizes the need to consider the robustness of foundation models such as SAM before deploying deep learning models in applications where security is critical.

Patent Mining by Extracting Functional Analysis Information Modelled As Graph Structure: A Patent Knowledge-base Collaborative Building Approach

2023-04-29 17:34:11+00:00

['Manal E. Helal']

As innovations in design continue to emerge, patents provide a wealth of information, and patent mining is a powerful tool to harness this knowledge. The process of mining patents involves a combination of technological techniques, such as text mining, natural language processing, machine learning, and ontology-building techniques. Recently, a research paper titled "Patent Mining by Extracting Functional Analysis Information Modelled as Graph Structure: A Patent Knowledge-base Collaborative Building Approach" proposed an automated graph data modelling method for extracting functional representations of mechanical design patents to build a comprehensive patent knowledge-base. The proposed method employs a schema-free graph modelling approach to enable the ontology to evolve, generalise to upper ontologies and specify lower ontologies. This graph modelling approach also enables efficient storage and enhanced performance of deep queries across many levels of relationships and interactions, providing for efficient data retrieval. Additionally, graph modelling libraries allow for immediate visualisation without the need for graph extraction programs from relational databases. The proposed method utilises advanced search query techniques, employing counting overlaps of different levels and weights, for patent/design comparisons. The research team was able to produce the PatMine SolidWorks Add-in, which is capable of highlighting overlapping design concepts between annotated CAD designs and patents. The patent annotation extracts functional analysis, representing a patent's structure as geometric feature interactions. The technology of the PatMine patents database combines full-text search and semantic search features while allowing for the implementation of graph-analytic methods and machine learning algorithms as plug-ins in future works. In conclusion, this research presents a robust and practical method for extracting functional analysis of mechanical design patents. By using graph modelling techniques, efficient storage and performance of deep queries across many levels of relationships and interactions are achieved, enabling a comprehensive patent knowledge-base that can evolve and generalise to upper ontologies. With the PatMine SolidWorks Add-in, annotated CAD designs can be compared with patents, indicating overlapping design concepts, thus providing a powerful tool to inform the design process in the future.

pyBibX -- A Python Library for Bibliometric and Scientometric Analysis Powered with Artificial Intelligence Tools

2023-04-27 20:06:07+00:00

['Valdecy Pereira', 'Marcio Pereira Basilio', 'Carlos Henrique Tarjano Santos']

PyBibX is a python library that enables researchers to conduct comprehensive bibliometric and scientometric analyses on raw data files sourced from Scopus, Web of Science, and PubMed, using state-of-the-art AI capabilities and advanced network analysis, citation analysis, collaboration analysis, and similarity analysis. Researchers can use this library to conduct various natural language processing tasks, including text summarization, topic modeling, and embedding vectors. The library's built-in EDA features produce visually appealing graphical illustrations, enabling researchers to identify patterns and trends quickly. As a demonstration, the researchers analyzed 184 documents associated with multiple-criteria decision analysis published between 1984 and 2023. The analysis highlighted the growing fascination with decision-making and fuzzy logic methodologies. Additionally, the network analysis accentuated the significance of central authors and intra-continental collaboration, identifying Canada and China as crucial collaboration hubs. They also employed AI analysis, which distinguished two primary topics and chatGPT preeminence in text summarization, proving it to be an indispensable instrument for interpreting results, as researchers could pose inquiries to chatGPT regarding bibliometric outcomes. PyBibX integrates cutting-edge AI capabilities with bibliometric and scientometric analyses, enabling researchers to interpret outcomes more effectively. However, data homogeneity remains a daunting challenge due to inconsistencies in databases. PyBibX is the first application of its kind, and researchers can use it to examine and interpret bibliometric and scientometric data rigorously.
